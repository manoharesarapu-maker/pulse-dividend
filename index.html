Sales enablement · how to use the City Dividend calculator

Run a value conversation, not a product demo.

This tab gives sales teams the talk track, discovery flow, calculation explanation and safe language needed to use the CityPulse City Dividend calculator with council CEOs, CIOs, Directors, Finance, Infrastructure, Community, Economy/Place and Operations leaders.

Purpose

What the tool is

A configurable value engine that converts council pain points into a transparent City Dividend across Financial, Time, Community, Economic, Climate and Safety outcomes.

Positioning

What the tool is not

It is not a guaranteed ROI promise, a finance-only spreadsheet, or a fine-revenue machine. It is a structured value hypothesis to validate with council data.

Mindset

The golden rule

Let the council own the assumptions. The strongest sales moment is not a high number; it is a trusted number the council recognises.

The simple sales talk track

“Let’s start with the operating pressures you are already feeling — parking, kerbside pressure, asset condition, community sentiment, economic vitality, safety, climate or reporting. We then configure CityPulse around those pressures and make every assumption visible so your leaders can challenge, tune and validate the value case.”

How to run the conversation

Start with pain, not products.Ask where the council is losing time, revenue, trust, safety confidence, economic activity or asset visibility.
Select the council profile.Use it as a starting point only. Explain that profiles seed assumptions; they are not benchmarks carved into stone tablets.
Select pain points.Choose only the pressures that genuinely apply. A smaller, credible value story beats a Christmas tree of every possible benefit.
Review product use cases.Explain how CityPulse, SenFORCE, SenPIC, SenMAP, SenDISA, SenBOS, SenIQ, Community and Economic Intelligence combine.
Tune assumptions openly.Invite the customer to challenge every value: uplift rates, cost per hour, event attendance, spend attribution, maturity and deployment scale.
Interpret the City Dividend.Separate validated financial value from broader council value. Do not collapse trust, safety and economic vitality into “cash savings.”
Finish with validation.Agree the data owners needed for a validation workshop: Finance, Local Laws, Assets, Community, Economy/Place, Operations and ICT.

Calculation & assumptions field guide · practical coaching for new sales users

Know what to enter, what to ask, and how each number changes the value case.

This guide is deliberately practical. It explains the calculator from the point of view of a new salesperson sitting with a council officer: what each input means, where the data comes from, what questions to ask before entering it, and how to explain the result without overclaiming.

Release hardening note: This guide is the field manual for a new sales user. The same knowledge is also used by tooltips, the help drawer, and the SenIQ Advisor so the calculator teaches users at the point of use.
Step 1

Inputs

Inputs are the council-specific numbers entered into the model: revenue, volumes, FTE, costs, cameras, vehicles, events, complaints and activity levels.

Step 2

Assumptions

Assumptions are improvement rates or factors such as uplift %, collection rate, deployment scale, maturity, scenario and attribution. These should be discussed openly.

Step 3

Outputs

Outputs include the City Dividend, financial view, NPV, BCR, payback, cashability split, product costs and assumptions register.

The calculation flow

1. Start with council pain.Select pressures the council recognises: kerbside conflict, asset uncertainty, community escalation, event/economic vitality, safety, climate or reporting burden.
2. Activate relevant CityPulse use cases.Pain points turn on use cases across SenFORCE, SenPIC, SenMAP, SenDISA, SenBOS, Community Intelligence, Economic Intelligence, Reports and SenIQ.
3. Enter local values.Use real council data where possible. If data is not available, enter a conservative estimate and mark it for validation.
4. Apply scenarios and maturity.Scenario and maturity settings adjust the value to reflect adoption, readiness and confidence. These are editable by admin users.
5. Calculate benefits and costs.Each benefit line uses a visible formula. Product costs include annual and one-off components where configured.
6. Explain the value split.Separate cashable/avoided-cost value from time, risk, economic, community, climate and safety value. Do not call everything savings.

The master formula pattern

Annual benefit = addressable value × impact assumption × deployment scale × scenario multiplier × maturity multiplier × ramp/adoption factor

Different modules use different versions of this pattern. SenFORCE uses revenue and coverage assumptions. SenPIC uses site and use-case assumptions. SenMAP uses asset and inspection assumptions. Community Intelligence uses complaints, sentiment and escalation assumptions. Economic Intelligence uses events, footfall, spend and attribution assumptions.

Field-by-field guidance: what to ask before entering values

Annual parking / compliance revenue

Meaning: current annual collected revenue from parking, kerbside or compliance infringements.

Ask: “What was actual collected parking/compliance revenue last financial year?”

Source: Finance, infringements system, Local Laws/Parking reports.

Mistake to avoid: do not use gross issued value if collection and withdrawals are material.

Infringement volume / year

Meaning: number of infringements issued or processed per year.

Ask: “How many infringement events were issued, withdrawn, paid and appealed last year?”

Use: drives back-office processing, evidence and dispute workload assumptions.

Collection rate

Meaning: proportion of issued infringement value actually collected.

Ask: “Of issued infringement value, what percentage is actually paid after withdrawals and write-offs?”

Safe entry: use a conservative net collection rate unless Finance provides a validated number.

Appeal / withdrawal rate

Meaning: share of infringements appealed, withdrawn, cancelled or requiring dispute handling.

Ask: “How many infringements are challenged and how much officer time does each dispute take?”

Use: supports SenBOS/evidence workflow time-saving value.

SenFORCE deployment scale factor

Meaning: how much of the addressable mobile compliance opportunity the proposed deployment can realistically cover.

Ask: “How many vehicles, patrol days, routes, hotspots and peak periods will actually be covered?”

Suggested range: pilot 0.25–0.40; regular priority coverage 0.40–0.60; mature hotspot coverage 0.60–0.80; broad disciplined deployment 0.80–1.00.

Mistake: do not assume two vehicles simply doubles value; high-yield areas are usually covered first.

SenFORCE addressable uplift

Meaning: the share of current revenue base that could be improved through better mobile coverage and evidence.

Ask: “Where is revenue or compliance currently leaking due to limited patrol coverage, event peaks or weak evidence?”

Safe entry: keep conservative until route and hotspot data is validated.

SenPIC fixed sites total

Meaning: total proposed fixed-location SenPIC sites, including compliance, occupancy, footfall, event and monitoring uses.

Ask: “Which locations require continuous visibility that manual patrols cannot provide?”

Important: SenPIC is not just school zones. It can support loading zones, disability bays, timed parking, permit zones, event footfall, occupancy and public realm hotspots.

SenPIC enforcement-enabled sites

Meaning: fixed SenPIC sites where enforcement is legally, operationally and policy enabled.

Ask: “Which locations have the authority, signage, delegation, process and evidence pathway to issue infringements?”

Mistake: do not count monitoring-only or insight-only sites as enforcement-enabled.

Event / footfall sites

Meaning: fixed SenPIC sites used to count people, occupancy, crowds or event activity.

Ask: “Which precincts or event locations need attendance, crowd or footfall evidence?”

Use: supports Economic Intelligence, event ROI, safety and operational planning.

Reactive maintenance spend

Meaning: annual unplanned maintenance expenditure responding to failures, complaints and emergency repairs.

Ask: “How much is reactive versus planned maintenance, and which asset classes drive emergency work?”

Mistake: do not claim CityPulse removes the maintenance budget; it targets the avoidable component.

Inspection FTE

Meaning: staff effort used for field inspections, asset audits and condition checking.

Ask: “How many officers spend material time inspecting roads, paths, signs, public realm or defects?”

Use: supports time/capacity value, not automatically cash savings.

Reporting effort

Meaning: officer time used to prepare recurring reports, briefings, councillor updates and operational summaries.

Ask: “Who prepares recurring reports, how often, and how many hours are spent gathering and formatting information?”

Use: supports Report Engine and SenIQ capacity value.

Formal complaints / escalation risk

Meaning: service issues, community complaints or requests that can escalate to councillors, executives or media.

Ask: “Which complaint types become politically or reputationally sensitive when council acts too late?”

Use: supports Community Intelligence early-warning value.

Early detection and escalation avoidance

Meaning: estimated share of issues detected early and the share of those where escalation can realistically be avoided.

Ask: “If council had seen this issue earlier, how often could it have prevented escalation?”

Safe entry: be conservative; these assumptions are usually medium or low confidence until measured.

Events, attendance and visitor share

Meaning: event volume, average attendance and proportion of attendees from outside the LGA.

Ask: “Which events matter economically, how many people attend, and what evidence exists?”

Use: supports Economic Intelligence and event ROI value.

Average visitor spend and attribution

Meaning: estimated spend per visitor and the share of that value reasonably attributable to better CityPulse-informed operations.

Ask: “What is the source for spend assumptions and what part can reasonably be influenced by parking, safety, event planning or precinct operations?”

Mistake: do not claim total event spend as council revenue.

Outputs: what each number means

OutputMeaningHow to explain it safely
Annual Total City DividendTotal estimated annual benefit across selected financial, time, community, economic, climate and safety benefits, before selected product costs.“This is the full annual public-value picture, not just direct budget savings.”
Annual Validated Financial BenefitAnnual financially-oriented benefit before selected product costs, usually cashable revenue plus avoided cost.“This is the part to validate most closely with Finance and service owners.”
NPVFuture net benefits discounted into today’s value.“A positive NPV means modelled benefits exceed costs after discounting future years.”
BCRDiscounted benefits divided by discounted costs.“Above 1 means benefits exceed costs. Always explain which benefit categories are included.”
PaybackApproximate time for cumulative net value to recover upfront and recurring costs.“Useful as a simple signal, but it depends on deployment timing, ramp and one-off costs.”
CashabilityWhether a benefit is cashable, avoided cost, capacity, risk-adjusted or strategic.“Capacity released and economic value should not be described as guaranteed cash savings.”

Common mistakes to avoid

Good practice

  • Use council-supplied data where available.
  • Start conservative and let the council increase assumptions.
  • Separate cashable, avoided-cost, capacity and broader public value.
  • Validate high-value and low-confidence assumptions first.
  • Use SenIQ Advisor when unsure what a field means.

Do not do this

  • Do not count monitoring-only SenPIC sites as enforcement sites.
  • Do not treat total event spend as council revenue.
  • Do not assume each extra vehicle or camera gives equal value.
  • Do not call officer time released a budget saving unless there is a cashable pathway.
  • Do not hide uncertain assumptions.

Admin guide · internal model-owner configuration

Change assumptions safely without changing the code.

This tab is for internal model owners and advanced users. It explains what can be changed, what should be validated, and how to avoid breaking the value model. Customer-facing users should rely on the Calculator, Calculation Guide and SenIQ Advisor.

What admins can change from the Admin panel

  • App title, subtitle, discount rate and time horizon.
  • Scenario names, multipliers and descriptions.
  • Maturity names, multipliers and descriptions.
  • Assumption values such as revenue, volumes, rates, FTE, sites and deployment factors.
  • Product cost inputs including licence, hardware, implementation, installation and support assumptions.
  • Benefit formulas, cashability labels, confidence labels and ramp profiles.
  • Full JSON configuration import/export for controlled model updates.

What requires extra care

  • Changing formulas can materially alter the investment case. Review formulas before external use.
  • Changing scenario or maturity multipliers affects all benefit lines.
  • Changing product costs affects payback, NPV and BCR immediately.
  • Changing attribution assumptions can overstate broader public value if not validated.
  • Changing field definitions or help text should be aligned with the Calculation Guide and SenIQ Advisor.

Recommended model-owner workflow

1. Duplicate the current version.Keep a clean master copy before making major config changes.
2. Change one assumption family at a time.For example, update SenFORCE assumptions first, then SenPIC, then Economic Intelligence.
3. Run the Clearwater demo.Check that the example still produces sensible numbers and the payback does not look unrealistic.
4. Check the Assumptions and Formula registers.Make sure formulas are explainable and high-value assumptions are not hidden.
5. Export the config JSON.Keep versioned config snapshots so assumptions can be traced and restored.

Admin caution

This is a single-file Phase 1 site. The Admin panel is a configuration convenience, not secure access control. Do not store confidential customer data or sensitive pricing in a public copy. For production use, configuration, authentication, saved scenarios and document generation should move to a controlled backend.
